FLOW 3

BUILD AN APP

You need to design a prototype for a mobile app. You will need Adobe XD to demonstrate the screens in the app and the user's flow. The project is prepared in groups (the groups are set in advance). You must choose a company or organization and imagine that they want to go to market with a product or service, where the interaction with one or more of the UN's world goals is an essential element in the product's quality for the customer. You must of course choose a company and a product where it makes sense to create an app and where one or more of the UN's world goals fit into the concept. Read more about the UN's 17 world goals here: www.verdensmaalene.dk

The app must be developed for both mobile and tablet and there must be content in at least 3 menu items and at least 3 levels. The content must be sufficient for user testing to be carried out, but you are welcome to supplement with generic content, placeholder text or text that you copy from other sources. You must do your best to create a visual design that appeals as much as possible to a specific audience. Make well-considered choices in terms of style, color choices, icons and fundamental design principles. You need to test whether users understand your design. You must investigate how you best organize the content in the app, using the test method short sorting, as well as whether the users understand how the app works, by conducting think-aloud tests on the potential target group. As a group, you must prepare an oral presentation, where you review the development of your product from the first hand-drawn sketches to digital prototype. You must explain how you have arrived at your solution and argue for the choices (and opt-outs) you have made along the way.

  • Early visualization (including sketches and wireframes)
  • Information architecture (including sitemap, flowchart, results from card sorting test)
  • Target group description (including personas)
  • Presentation of visual design (in relation to target group)
  • Test results (including think aloud test)
  • Brief review of prototype
